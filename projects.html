🐝 projects

Here are some of the personal projects I've made either to learn something or just for fun.

NYC COVID Tracker

During fall of 2020, New York required 2-week quarantines for travelers from designated 'hotspot' states. However, the criteria for a 'hotspot' state was confusing and the list of 'hotspot' states changed every week. As a result, I made this tracker to show how close states were to being called a 'hotspot' in order to help NYU students make decisions about whether or not it was worth to go home for the holidays.

Boba Tracker

One of my new year's resolutions for 2021 was to drink less boba. To do so, I made a webapp where I could track all the times I drank boba throughout 2021. It would tell me how much money I've spent, how many times I've drank boba, and when the last time I got it was. By doing so, I discourage myself from getting it too often and also get to see data about my favorite drinks and stores.

USA Covid Tracker

During Fall 2020, NYU administration allowed students to go home for winter break. However, New York covid guidelines required that travelers from certain states had to quarantine for 14 days.

However, these "certain" states were often changing. New York stated that states with high rates of covid, along with some other guidelines, put them on a "quarantine" list. This list was updated every week.

This is a problem, since winter break is only 1 month long. If students were to go home, they would need to know when they must return so that they can quarantine in time for Spring 2021 classes.

Therefore, my tool solves this problem by showing how each state's covid rates compared to the New York guidelines. It helped students make decisions about whether or not it was worth to go home for the holidays.
